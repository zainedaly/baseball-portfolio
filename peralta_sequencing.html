04 — Scouting Report

HOW TO ATTACK

Data-driven recommendations for opposing hitters and advance scouts preparing against Peralta.

⚔
Exploitable Tendencies
Sit on the Slider → Curveball Pattern
When Peralta throws back-to-back breaking balls, hitters have his worst sequence at 17.9% good outcome rate. Look for the slider first, then expect the curveball and drive it.
Attack After the Changeup
Pitches following the changeup show his lowest good outcome rates across the board. The changeup → fastball (32.3%) and changeup → slider (30.2%) are hittable when you know they're coming.
Take the First Pitch
With a 53.8% fastball usage rate, Peralta establishes the heater early. Working deep into counts forces him to use his secondary stuff — where hitters have more success.
🛡
Pitcher Strengths
Slider → Changeup is Untouchable
His best sequence generates a 23.4% whiff rate — nearly double his overall rate. Hitters must respect both directions of break simultaneously, which is nearly impossible.
Curveball Sets Up Everything
The curveball is the most effective setup pitch in his arsenal. After showing it, every subsequent pitch — especially the fastball — plays up significantly due to eye-level disruption.
05 — Methodology

HOW THIS WAS BUILT

A transparent look at the data pipeline and modeling approach behind this analysis.

01
Data Collection
2024 MLB Statcast data pulled via the pybaseball Python library — 731,904 total pitches across the full season. Filtered to 9 primary pitch types with meaningful sample sizes, yielding 715,429 clean pitches.
02
Feature Engineering
A "previous pitch" feature was engineered by shifting pitch sequences within each pitcher's outing. Good outcomes defined as whiffs (swinging strike) or weak contact (exit velocity <85 mph). Class imbalance corrected via balanced class weights.
03
Model Training
Random Forest classifier trained on 8 features: count (balls/strikes), outs, inning, previous pitch, pitch thrown, and batter handedness. Feature importance analysis confirmed previous pitch as the #2 predictor of good outcomes league-wide.
